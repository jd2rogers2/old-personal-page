Why did I decide to learn software development?

In short... because I wasn’t being challenged at my last position, so I decided to start figuring out what I wanted to do.

It starts when I picked the wrong major and the wrong school and breezed through college with minimal effort. Then I finally got a job in real estate but, as I said, wasn’t being challenged. I dipped my feet in a lot of different areas such as GRE studies, series 7 studies, building a PC, and ended up really enjoying the PC.

After lots of research on how each component works, I finally felt like I had a good idea of what I wanted to buy. Then a day or two of tinkering, maybe I had to buy one more component I can’t remember, and then downloading Linux; and I was done! It’s a really cool experience, using a computer that you built. Naturally I wanted to continue, so I poked around on some MOOC Java classes and CodeAcademy and what not. After a while of that, it was time to take the plunge, so I started researching full time classes.

Obviously a big part of my decision was the return on investment. I’d get to be a part of an industry with massive growth and that is the cool thing right now; the salary bump wasn’t bad either, and all for a minimal investment.

I had been saving for at least a year already, since I knew I was going to be making a career change. So a little sacrifice there in the form of reduced spending, mixed with the opportunity cost of no income for 3-6 months, tuition, and (again) 3-6 months of hard work; but that’s it, that’s really all of the expenses.

Flatiron was a no brainer. No other bootcamps have released jobs reports or offer tuition reimbursement, and the price for the online Learn Verified program can’t be beaten.
